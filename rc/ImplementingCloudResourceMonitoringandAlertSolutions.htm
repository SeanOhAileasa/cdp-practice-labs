Exercise 1 - Examining and Configuring Monitoring Alerts and Service Management Tools

Monitoring alerts is the methodology in which administrators are notified when a resource is not operating at a baseline. Alerts play an important role in monitoring environments as they help you react to issues as they arise.

Service Management Tools help organise multiple issues in the form of help desk tickets. Help desk tickets can be assigned to different members of a team and allows you to know who is working on which issues in an environment.

Monitoring alerts can be integrated with Service Management Tools to create help desk tickets as problems arise. This is the zen of monitoring your environment and something you should strive to achieve.

Alerts can be sent via email, SMS text message, phone calls, or trigger applications to perform a certain task. You can define events to trigger these alerting actions, such as system up or down status, CPU usage, RAM usage, storage usage and many other events.
